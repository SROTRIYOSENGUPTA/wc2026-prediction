Key Storylines from the Model

The Predicted Final Is Real: Spain vs Argentina
The bracket the model has been predicting is now reality: Spain beat France 2–0 in the semifinal it had flagged as a 52–48 coin flip, and face Argentina in the Final — the exact matchup projected since the group stage. The sim gives Spain 66% / Argentina 34%: tournament-best ELO and a watertight defence against Messi's hot hand.
💥
Model Report Card — 25 of 31 Knockout Calls Correct
Through 31 knockout games the model has called 25 winners correctly (81%). Of the six misses, two went to penalties after 120 level minutes (Germany, Netherlands), and three were games it had flagged near coin-flip (Brazil–Norway 56%, Mexico–England 65%, Norway–England 55%). The one true shock: England's 6–4 bronze win over a France side it had at 79.8%.
🇲🇦
The Giant-Killers — Paraguay, Morocco, Norway
The tournament's upsets came in threes: Paraguay knocked out Germany on penalties, Morocco did the same to the Netherlands and reached the quarterfinal, and Norway — Haaland leading — beat Brazil to reach the semis. None survived the final four, but they reshaped every half of the bracket the model had to re-simulate.
🇳🇴
Norway Stun Brazil — but England End the Dream
The Round-of-16 shock: Norway beat Brazil 2–1 and knocked the five-time champions out — Haaland's men the story of the right half. But the coin-flip quarterfinal went the other way: England won 2–1 and ended the dream. England now face Argentina in a 49–51 semifinal — the tightest game left on the board.
🇫🇷
France Fall in the Semis — Bronze Game vs England
France entered the semis as the marginal favourite (37.3%) on Mbappé's Golden Boot charge — and lost the coin flip: Spain won 2–0. It's the modal-vs-marginal lesson in one game: the most-likely single path (Spain's) beat the most-likely champion (France). Their tournament ended with one more twist: England won the bronze game 6–4 — a ten-goal thriller the model had France 79.8% to win. France finish fourth.
📊
One Game Left — Spain 66/34 in the Final
Both semifinals were model coin flips and both broke chalk: Spain 2–0 France, Argentina 2–1 England. England then snatched bronze 6–4. One game remains — the 500k sim reduces to a single number: Spain 66.2% · Argentina 33.8%. Spain's rating and defence versus Messi, one game for the title, exactly the Final this model called from the start.
