Key Storylines from the Model
🏆
Spain Win It — 66.5% Over Argentina in the Final
The model makes Spain the single most-likely champion: past Portugal and Belgium, then France in the semifinal (59.1%), then 66.5% over Argentina in the Final. Their 3–0 dismantling of Austria in the R32 (Oyarzabal 2, Porro), tournament-best ELO and Yamal's form carry the chalk path all the way.
💥
Germany Are OUT — Paraguay Stun Them on Penalties
The shock of the Round of 32 already happened: Paraguay held Germany 1–1 and won 4–3 on penalties, dumping a pre-tournament dark horse out at the first knockout hurdle. The bracket above locks that result — Paraguay take Germany's place against France in the Round of 16, where their run finally ends.
🇲🇦
Morocco Stun the Netherlands on Penalties
The second Round-of-32 shock is locked in too: Morocco held the Netherlands 1–1 and won 3–2 on penalties (Diop 90+1'), knocking a genuine contender out at the first hurdle. The bracket reroutes around it — Morocco take the Dutch slot against Canada, and reach the quarterfinal before France end the run.
🇧🇷
Brazil's Form Carries Them to the Semis
Brazil opened with a 2–1 win over Japan and carry the tournament's second-best form (z = +1.27) — enough to make them the right-half's deepest run: past Norway and host Mexico, into the semifinals, where Argentina edge them 56.9%. They then fall to France in the bronze final and take fourth.
🇫🇷
France Top the Title Odds — but Exit in the Semis
France opened with a ruthless 3–0 over Sweden (Mbappé 2) and top the Monte Carlo title odds at 25.6% — yet on the single most-likely path Spain's higher ELO edges them 59.1% in the semifinal. They beat Brazil for bronze. The gap between "most likely to win the tournament" and "wins this exact bracket" is the whole story (see below).
📊
Modal vs Marginal — Why Spain Is Chalk but France Lead the Odds
The bracket shows the modal path: advance the favourite in every single tie, and you get Spain as champion. The 500k Monte Carlo instead integrates over every upset, giving marginal title odds: France 25.6% · Spain 20.0% · Argentina 14.0% · England 8.4% · Brazil 8.2% · Portugal 4.3%. France win more often across all worlds; Spain wins this one most-likely bracket. Both are true.
