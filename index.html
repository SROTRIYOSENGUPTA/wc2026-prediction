The "Death Half" — France, Spain & Portugal All Left
France, Spain, and Portugal are all in the same bracket half — only one reaches the Final. Spain beats Portugal R16 (56.1%), Belgium QF (80.9%), then defeats France in the Semi-Final (57.8%). Dembélé hat-trick vs Norway elevated France's ELO — making Spain's half harder than ever.
🇫🇷
France 4–1 Norway: Dembélé Hat-Trick Reshapes the Bracket
France's dominant group stage — 4–1 Norway (Dembélé hat-trick), 3–0 Iraq, 3–1 Senegal — vaulted them to 15.21% tournament win probability (up from 11.64%). Norway's ELO drop means Ivory Coast now beats them R32 (61.6%). The updated model elevates France as the second most likely champion.
⭐
Yamal Effect: Spain Beats Portugal 56.1% in R16
Lamine Yamal, Ballon d'Or #2 (score 0.965), gives Spain a decisive edge over Portugal in R16 — 56.1% Spain. Spain kept three consecutive clean sheets in the group stage. The model's Ballon d'Or feature captures what ELO and xG alone cannot: the difference a generational talent makes in knockout football.
🔥
Spain Beats Argentina in the Final — 70.0%
Spain meets Argentina in the Final — opposite halves of the bracket, only one path. Spain's Yamal-led brilliance plus dominant group stage gives them a 70.0% edge. Argentina edges England in a tense SF (59.1%) to get there. Messi vs Yamal — the old master vs the new king.

England Stuns Brazil in QF — 78.9%
Brazil drew Morocco 1–1 in their opener — a significant ELO hit. After beating Ivory Coast in R16 (78.2%), Brazil runs into England in the QF. The model gives England a remarkable 78.9% probability — Brazil's tournament xG numbers and ELO deficit make this less of a shock than it looks.
🇦🇷
Argentina Leads 500k Simulation at 16.94%
Updated with live WC2026 results: Argentina leads the 500k Monte Carlo at 16.94% (France 15.21%, Netherlands 11.24%, Spain 10.20%). Messi's 5-goal group stage haul — setting the all-time WC goals record at 18 — plus MLS host familiarity boost makes Argentina the simulation's #1 pick.
